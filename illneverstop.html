*********
I’ll Never Stop Loving You
*********

"I wonder if we’ll get used to each other..."
As if love were a trick that time unmakes.
But darling what a privilege,
to know the map of your sighs,
to memorize the way your silence speaks.
Let me get used to you
the way a poet gets used to heartbreak,
not because it’s painless
but because it’s worth the ache.

"What if routine makes us forget what we have?"
What if magic turns into habit?
Then I will make a ritual of wonder.
I’ll find galaxies in your morning breath,
constellations in your tired eyes,
and still ask, years in,
"How did I get so lucky?"

"What if we stop doing the little things?"
When the world grows heavy,
and romance fades beneath bills and broken sleep.
Then I’ll leave you love notes
on receipts and fogged mirrors,
and draw constellations on your back
just to hear you breathe differently.

"What if one day… I don’t feel the same?"
Then I’ll love you harder on purpose.
I won’t wait for fireworks.
I’ll be the quiet match in the dark,
the slow-burning ember that never dies,
warming the parts of you even you forgot were cold.

"What if we stop trying?"
Then I’ll try enough for both of us.
I’ll fight for us on tired Tuesday nights,
hold your hand through silences too loud,
and choose you
even when love feels less like a storm,
and more like still water.

"What if the madness fades?"
Then let it.
Let it become reverence.
Let it become a soft turning of pages,
the whisper of a thumb brushing a ring.
Let love grow old with us
wrinkled, wise, and unwavering.

Because darling, routine is not the end.
It’s the quiet promise beneath the noise.
And if I must get used to something,
let it be your shadow beside mine at dusk,
your voice behind every door I open.
Let me get used to your yawns,
your late-night ramblings, your morning complaints.
Let me get used to your everything.

Let our love be no accident
Let it be a daily decision,
etched in cracked lips and tired arms,
in forgiveness and second chances,
in burnt toast and shared sunsets.

Even when the world forgets how to love,
I will remember.
I will remind you.
And on the days we feel less than magic
I’ll look you in the eyes,
and whisper like a vow:

"I still choose you."
~Your Forever
