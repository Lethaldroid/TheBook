/**
What Is Beauty, If Not You
**/

What is beauty,
if not you?
If not the way your voice
feels like a meadow before wind,
or how your laughter
cracks silence like sunlight through shutters.

The world tried to teach me beauty
through paintings, poetry, spring’s bloom
but I didn’t learn
until I met you.

Now, when petals unfurl,
I see your fingers,
gentle as rain on glass.
When dawn gilds the clouds,
I remember your skin
kissed by light,
as if morning was made to reflect you.

Soon, miles will fold
between your breath and mine.
I’ll walk under foreign skies,
cradle silence in new winds—
but even then:

I’ll see you in the snow,
each flake a whisper of your touch.
In Finland’s endless white,
you’ll bloom in my memory
like wildflowers in frost.

When auroras dance,
they won’t dazzle—
they’ll remind me
of your eyes,
your spirit,
of that one night you looked at me
like I was worth loving.

What is beauty,
if not the ache of missing you
in everything breathtaking?

You are the measure
of all that is lovely.
The poem that writes itself
into every landscape I touch.

And if they ask me what beauty is,
I’ll say—
it’s how she looked at me
like I was her home,
just before I had to leave.

You're the pulse behind every poem I'll write
just to survive your absence.
you are not just beautiful-
you are why beauty exists.

--- END OF POEM ---
