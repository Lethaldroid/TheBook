Where I End and You Begin
--------------------------------

I want to climb into your lap,
spill my love between kisses,
interrupt words with the press of my lips
until I waste away in your arms.

I would peel my skin back
just to be closer when you hold me,
break my ribs, part the muscle,
let my heart feel more of you—
let it beat beneath your touch.

I would make a home out of you,
dance through its corridors forever,
never longing, never leaving,
only living in the warmth of you.

I want to do everything with you—
sleep, sing, brush our teeth,
shower, travel, make dinner,
ride bikes like children,
walk paths leading nowhere but us.

I would crack myself open like a fault line
if it meant the trembling earth between us
could settle into stillness.

I would crawl into the spaces
between your breaths,
curl into the hollow of your throat,
live there, safe, surrounded by you.

If you asked, I would pour myself into you
until the lines between us
blurred into nothing—
until you could wear me like a second skin
and never feel this world alone.

I would break the sky open,
pour its light into your hands,
just to watch its glow upon your skin.

I would slip between your ribs,
settle beside your heart,
so with every beat,
you would hear me whisper—
I love you.

Yours Only,
the puzzle piece

END OF POEM
