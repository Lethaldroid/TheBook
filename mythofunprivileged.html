*********
The Myth of Unprivileged
*********

There was once an infinite void—Chaos
Something similar existed inside me too,
But your beauty,
Started a world within me, completely anew.

Like Cronus trying to escape his fate,
I wish we could too,
Become suspended in the moments,
That feel eternal.

Tell me, Atlas, what is heavier?
The world upon your shoulders, or the weight of my heart?

Icarus soared too high, burned by ambition,
But with you, I find the balance that he missed.
Like Prometheus gifting fire to mankind,
You ignite within me a flame that endures.
Pandora opened her box and set free all torments,
Yet hope remains with me, shaped in the form of you.

Apollo’s pursuit of Daphne fades in comparison,
For I chase no fleeting beauty, but something far deeper.
And Narcissus—so lost in his reflection—
Never knew the joy of seeing his lover’s eyes.

Like Pygmalion with his perfect sculpture,
Fates got me a Psyche—
With a soul that completes me.

Eos asked for Tithonus’s eternal life,
But I will never make the mistake,
I would never do that as I,
Would wish for eternal youth,
And an eternity with you.

Dionysus gave Midas a curse; disguised as a gift,
I think you have that touch too,
Rather a blessing than a curse,
For your touch, turned my life to gold.

But when the earth darkened,
With Persephone’s absence,
My world would wither too— if we grow distant.
Like winter, cold and lifeless, devoid of bloom.

Tantalus suffered eternal thirst and hunger,
And I shall too, for only if I knew,
When would it be the right moment—the right time,
To tell you.

Sisyphus pushed his boulder,
But for me, it was never in vain.
Because you are the purpose
That Sisyphus forever seeks.

In the Fields of Mourning, where broken hearts once lingered,
If that’s where you ever went to,
Like Orpheus descending for Eurydice,
I shall traverse the depths of hell,
for a single glance back at you.

Heracles, completing all of his impossible tasks,
For I shall complete mine too,
Even if I have to wait an eternity,
I will wait for you.

The Argonauts sailed for the golden fleece,
But I think they looked in the wrong place,
For you are the hidden treasure,
I'll always keep.

Talos had a weakness, a single nail in his ankle,
But you are the thorn in my heart, and who can pull that out?

Interestingly,
This tale, like the Greeks, feels like a myth too,
Like someone in a fantasy or fiction
That one can only dream of,
I would name this myth,
"The myth of Unprivileged"
