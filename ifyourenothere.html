If You're Not Here
**/

If you're not here
when the moonlight leans,
then stars will sigh
and forget their gleam.

If you don’t stay
when the morning sings,
then I’ll let go
of all the sweet things.

We’ve both been bruised,
worn by storms we didn’t choose,
and yet your sorrow mirrors mine—
two reflections in the same line.

I see me in you,
in the way you break,
the way you hope
through every ache.

Take the hush,
take the sky,
take the world
as you pass by—

But leave your love
beneath my skin,
so the piece I lost
can come back in.

Because we were never whole
until we found each other,
two quiet souls
woven into one another.

Your Other Half
