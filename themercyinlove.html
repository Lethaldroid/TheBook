*********
The Mercy in Love
*********

Love is not a shield.
It is the sword I hand you,
hilt first,
trusting you won’t turn the blade.

I undress my armor in your gaze,
knowing full well
you could kill the vulnerable man beneath
but you never do.

You touch like a storm that’s learned restraint,
hands trembling with power
you refuse to wield cruelly.

Your mouth
it could write elegies in blood,
could bruise,
could bite,
could leave proof of its conquest
on the canvas of my skin.

But instead,
it worships.

You hold me firmly,
not to silence
but to steady the moan
as I fall apart in your hands.

I ruin in your grasp,
and still,
you rebuild me with your breath.

There is mercy in my mouth.
There is safety in your surrender.
And I,
made of glass and longing,
would shatter gladly
knowing you’d gather every piece.

Because real love isn’t soft.
It is sharp.
But it never cuts without consent.
~The Naked Poet
